How I Work

  • I'm highly pragmatic and data-driven. I always want to see the data. There are times when you have to trust your gut to make certain decisions, but many decisions can be made by simply looking at the data.
  • I always look for ways to invoke the Pareto principle, identifying ways that we can simplify work.
  • Major initiatives and strategic decisions should have a short (up to 6 pages, ideally 1-2) document outlining them. Slides are great as a visual for big meetings, but documents are best for nearly everything else.
  • I'm big on using feature trackers like JIRA effectively. I also strongly believe in effective backlog refinement: sprints shouldn't be an endless waterfall.
  • I value transparency. If things aren't going well, I like to know just as much as when things are going well. We can work it out together. Similarly, I like to understand why questions are being asked; added context can unlock a better answer.
  • I used to work at Amazon and their leadership principles were ingrained in me, but one in particular sticks out: "disagree and commit". Sometimes we may disagree on how best to tackle something, but once the team gets bought in on something, it's important to fall in line and push forward.
  • I believe meetings should always have an agenda. We have so many meetings, so it's important to ensure that the ones we do have are 1) necessary, 2) properly allotted for time, and 3) have a clear purpose and outcome.
